The History of the Superstar

May 10, 2023 By Adidas Editorial Team 5 min read

The Adidas Superstar, with its iconic shell toe and three stripes, has transcended its basketball origins to become a cultural icon. First introduced in 1969 as a basketball shoe, the Superstar was designed to provide better support and protection for players' feet during games.

What made the Superstar truly revolutionary was its shell toe design. Unlike other basketball shoes of the era that featured canvas uppers and rubber toe caps, the Superstar introduced a leather upper and a protective rubber shell toe. This innovation not only provided better protection for players' toes but also increased the durability of the shoe.

Rise to Fame

By the 1970s, the Superstar had become the shoe of choice for many NBA players, with reportedly over 75% of all NBA players wearing the model. However, it was in the 1980s that the Superstar truly crossed over from the basketball court to the streets, largely thanks to hip-hop group Run-DMC.

Run-DMC's adoption of the Superstar as part of their signature look, worn without laces and with the tongue pushed out, helped to cement the shoe's place in popular culture. The group's 1986 song "My Adidas" further solidified the connection between the Superstar and hip-hop culture.

Cultural Icon

Today, the Superstar remains one of Adidas's most popular and recognizable shoes. It has been released in countless colorways and collaborations, from high-fashion designers to musicians and artists. Despite being over 50 years old, the Superstar continues to be a staple in sneaker culture and a symbol of street style.

The enduring popularity of the Superstar is a testament to its timeless design and cultural significance. From basketball courts to concert stages, from street corners to fashion runways, the Superstar has left an indelible mark on popular culture.
